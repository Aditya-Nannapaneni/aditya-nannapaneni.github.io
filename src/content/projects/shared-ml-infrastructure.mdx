## Context

Science and sales organizations repeatedly rebuilt training, evaluation, and deployment plumbing for similar ML problems. Duplication slowed delivery and made quality uneven.

## My contribution

I designed and delivered shared ML infrastructure components—pipelines, evaluation patterns, and reusable serving/training interfaces—that multiple teams adopted.

## System design

- **Inputs:** Datasets, model training jobs, evaluation configs, and deployment requests from internal teams.
- **Outputs:** Shared workflows for training, evaluation, and deployment.
- **Major components:** Reusable pipelines, evaluation harnesses, and operational defaults.
- **Deployment boundary:** Internal AWS environments.

This case study describes the system at a high level and excludes confidential AWS data, implementation details, and customer information.

## Key technical decisions

1. **Shared platform versus project-specific implementation** — shared defaults reduced duplicated operational cost.
2. **Evaluation baked into the platform** — teams inherited measurement patterns rather than inventing them per project.
3. **Adoption over completeness** — the platform prioritized the workflows teams actually ran.

## Evaluation

Adoption and productivity were measured through internal usage. Exact proprietary architecture and customer data are omitted.

## Results

Adopted by more than 30 scientists. Related work contributed to approximately 3× improvement in scientist productivity in approved reporting contexts where measured.

## What I learned

- Platform success is measured by adoption and time-to-reliable delivery.
- Good defaults beat optional best practices that nobody enables.
- Evaluation infrastructure is part of the product surface.
