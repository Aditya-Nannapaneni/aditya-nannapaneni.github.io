## Context

Teams needed to serve fine-tuned and adapter-based LLMs with predictable latency and acceptable cost. Project-specific serving stacks were duplicated, hard to operate, and expensive at scale.

## My contribution

I designed and delivered a reusable serving framework on top of vLLM with PEFT and LoRA support, including the interfaces, evaluation criteria, and operational patterns needed for shared adoption.

## System design

- **Inputs:** Model checkpoints, LoRA/PEFT adapters, serving configuration, and request traffic.
- **Outputs:** Low-latency completions with shared operational controls.
- **Major components:** vLLM runtime, adapter loading, request batching, and monitoring hooks.
- **Evaluation layer:** Throughput, latency, and cost comparisons against prior serving setups.
- **Deployment boundary:** Internal AWS serving environments used by multiple science teams.

This case study describes the system at a high level and excludes confidential AWS data, implementation details, and customer information.

## Key technical decisions

1. **Shared platform versus project-specific stacks** — a reusable serving layer reduced duplicated operational work.
2. **PEFT/LoRA-aware serving** — adapter support avoided full-model replication for many use cases.
3. **Quality versus latency and cost** — serving defaults were tuned for production traffic rather than research-only throughput.

## Evaluation

Compared the shared framework against prior serving setups using throughput, latency, and cost metrics under representative workloads. Exact customer traffic profiles and proprietary configuration details are omitted.

## Results

Approximately 5× improvement in serving performance across throughput, latency, and cost relative to the previous approach, at an approved reporting level.

## What I learned

- Adapter-aware serving changes the economics of multi-model deployment.
- Shared ML systems succeed when evaluation and operational defaults are part of the product, not afterthoughts.
- Cost and latency need the same rigor as model quality in production LLM systems.
