## Context

Sales and account teams needed better prioritization and decision support over large customer portfolios. Fragmented signals made it hard to act consistently at scale.

## My contribution

I designed and delivered core modeling and decision-intelligence components of the Large Customer Model, including evaluation and production integration patterns used by go-to-market teams.

## System design

- **Inputs:** Account and opportunity signals available to internal teams.
- **Outputs:** Prioritization and decision support used in pipeline workflows.
- **Major components:** Representation learning, ranking/decision layers, and explainability surfaces.
- **Deployment boundary:** Internal AWS production systems.

This case study describes the system at a high level and excludes confidential AWS data, implementation details, and customer information.

## Key technical decisions

1. **Decision support over raw prediction dumps** — outputs were shaped for operator workflows.
2. **Explainability as a product requirement** — adoption depended on inspectable recommendations.
3. **Shared platform components** — reused ML infrastructure rather than a one-off model stack.

## Evaluation

Evaluated with online and offline measures appropriate to the decision workflow. Proprietary datasets, customer identifiers, and internal architecture details are omitted.

## Results

Associated with a 15% improvement in pipeline velocity in NAMER, reported at an approved level.

## What I learned

- Production ML impact depends on workflow fit as much as model lift.
- Explainability can be a gating factor for adoption in sales systems.
- Shared infrastructure shortens the path from prototype to durable product.
